Our solar system

Diameter: 4,879 km Mass: 0.33 × 10²⁴ kg Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km Gravity: 3.7 m/s² Features: Mercury is the smallest planet with an extremely thin atmosphere. Its surface resembles that of Earth's Moon, and temperatures can swing from extremely hot to extremely cold due to the lack of atmosphere

Diameter: 12,756 km Mass: 5.97 × 10²⁴ kg Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km Gravity: 9.8 m/s² Features: Earth is the only planet known to support life, with a balanced atmosphere of nitrogen and oxygen, liquid water, and a stable climate conducive to life

Diameter: 6,792 km Mass: 0.64 × 10²⁴ kg Distance from Sun: 228 million km Gravity: 3.7 m/s² Features: Mars has a thin atmosphere mostly of carbon dioxide, surface conditions with the potential for ancient water flow, and features like canyons and volcanoes. It’s often targeted for future human exploration

Diameter: 120,536 km Mass: 568 × 10²⁴ kg Distance from Sun: 1.43 billion km Gravity: 9.0 m/s² Features: Saturn is famous for its extensive ring system made of ice and rock. Like Jupiter, it is a gas giant with many moons, including Titan, which has a thick atmosphere

Diameter: 49,528 km Mass: 102 × 10²⁴ kg Distance from Sun: 4.5 billion km Gravity: 11.0 m/s² Features: Neptune, also an ice giant, has strong winds and storms. It has a deep blue color and at least 16 moons. Its largest moon, Triton, is believed to be a captured dwarf planet
